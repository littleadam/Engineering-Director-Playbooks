# Engineering Onboarding Playbook
### Engineering Director Framework | Technical Lead Development

---

## The Problem

Most engineering onboarding programmes are built for individual contributors. They focus on environment setup, codebase orientation, and tool access.

Technical Lead onboarding is fundamentally different. A TL is not joining to execute tasks. A TL is joining to *own a domain* — and the sooner they own it, the sooner the programme benefits.

The failure modes of unstructured TL onboarding are expensive:

- The TL operates in shadow mode for months, afraid to make decisions
- The Director becomes the de facto TL because the new leader hasn't been given clear authority
- The team doesn't know who to go to — the old informal leader or the new official one
- The TL makes decisions without context and damages team trust in the first 30 days

This playbook builds structured accountability transfer — from Director oversight to TL ownership — across 90 days.

---

## The Framework: 30-60-90

### The Core Principle

The 30-60-90 model is not a checklist. It is an accountability transfer schedule.

At Day 0, the Director holds all accountability. By Day 90, the TL holds their domain accountability. The 90 days is the transfer period — structured, deliberate, and tracked.

---

### Day 1–30: Orient and Observe

**The TL's job in the first 30 days:** Understand before acting.

**What the Director owns in this phase:**
- All programme commitments and stakeholder relationships
- All escalation decisions
- Team structure and workload visibility

**What the TL builds in this phase:**

| Area | Activity | Output |
|---|---|---|
| Technical | Full codebase and architecture walkthrough with existing TLs | Architecture summary in their own words |
| Process | Shadow all sprint ceremonies — planning, review, retro | Written observations: what works, what doesn't |
| People | 1:1 with every engineer on their future team | Team map: strengths, blockers, working styles |
| Stakeholder | Introduced to (not leading) all stakeholder touchpoints | Stakeholder map with communication preferences |
| Systems | Full access to monitoring, CI/CD, incident tooling | Operational system summary |

**Day 30 checkpoint — Director-led review:**

The TL presents back:
1. How they understand the technical domain
2. What they observed about team health and delivery process
3. Who they see as key relationships — internal and external
4. Their initial hypothesis: what is working, what needs to change

The Director's role in this review: listen, correct misunderstandings, validate observations. Do not hand over accountability yet.

---

### Day 31–60: Participate and Lead Small

**The TL's job in Days 31–60:** Lead in low-risk contexts, with Director backup visible.

**What transfers to the TL in this phase:**
- Sprint planning facilitation (Director attends, does not lead)
- Day-to-day engineering decisions within the team
- First 1:1 ownership with direct reports
- Defect triage and P3 incident ownership

**What the Director retains:**
- Stakeholder communication on programme commitments
- P1/P2 incident escalation decisions
- Performance assessment and hiring decisions

**Structured activities for this phase:**

| Week | Focus | TL Action | Director Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| Week 5–6 | Sprint governance | Leads sprint planning and review | Attends, gives feedback post-session |
| Week 6–7 | Team 1:1s | Owns 1:1 cadence with all direct reports | Reviews 1:1 notes, coaches on approach |
| Week 7–8 | First delivery decision | Makes a real trade-off call (scope, timing, approach) | Validates the decision privately, does not override publicly |

**Day 60 checkpoint — Mutual assessment:**

Two conversations happen:
1. Director to TL: "Here is what I see working. Here is what I want you to develop in the next 30 days."
2. TL to Director: "Here is what I need from you to be effective. Here is what I am ready to own."

Honesty in this conversation determines the quality of Day 61–90.

---

### Day 61–90: Own and Be Accountable

**The TL's job in Days 61–90:** Lead as if the Director is not in the room.

**What fully transfers to the TL:**
- Sprint governance: planning, review, retrospective ownership
- Team-level stakeholder communication
- Engineering decisions within their domain
- P2 and P3 incident ownership and post-incident reviews
- Delivery commitments for their programme workstream

**What the Director continues:**
- Programme-level stakeholder relationships
- P1 incident oversight
- Cross-programme coordination
- TL performance coaching (weekly 1:1 continues)

**The test of Day 90:** The TL should be the person stakeholders contact first for their domain. Not the Director.

**Day 90 checkpoint — Formal accountability sign-off:**

The Director and TL co-present the programme status to senior leadership — with the TL leading their domain section. This is the formal transfer: the stakeholders now see the TL as the owner.

---

## The Weekly TL 1:1 — Director's Agenda

The weekly 1:1 is the most important investment a Director makes in a TL's development. It is not a status meeting. It is a coaching and accountability conversation.

**Agenda (30 minutes):**

```
DELIVERY (10 minutes)
- What is the team working on?
- Any risks to the sprint goal or release commitment?
- Any blockers the Director needs to clear?

PEOPLE (10 minutes)
- How is each engineer on the team doing?
- Any performance concerns emerging?
- Anyone who needs stretch, recognition, or support?

DEVELOPMENT (10 minutes)
- What decision did the TL make this week that they want to reflect on?
- What is one thing the Director observed that the TL should know?
- What is the TL working on for their own growth?
```

**Director principle:** The 1:1 agenda belongs to the TL. If the TL comes with nothing prepared, that is the coaching conversation.

## Performance Expectations by 90-Day Stage

| Competency | Day 30 | Day 60 | Day 90 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Technical domain knowledge | Understands architecture and key risks | Can make design recommendations | Owns technical decisions in their domain |
| Sprint governance | Observes and understands | Facilitates with coaching | Owns fully |
| Team management | Knows every engineer's name and role | Has completed one full 1:1 cycle | Is the team's primary leader |
| Stakeholder communication | Introduced and observing | Co-presenting with Director | Owns domain communication |
| Escalation quality | Learning the framework | Escalating with options | Consistently escalating decisions, not problems |

---

## Anti-Patterns to Eliminate

| Anti-Pattern | What It Looks Like | The Real Cost |
|---|---|---|
| Shadow mode too long | TL observes for 60+ days before leading | Team doesn't know who their leader is |
| Ownership without context | TL given full accountability before they understand the domain | Early decisions damage team trust |
| Director shadow | Director keeps answering questions the TL should answer | TL never builds credibility |
| Feedback hoarding | Director waits for 90-day review to share observations | TL misses 60 days of development opportunity |
| Skipping the Day 60 conversation | No honest mutual assessment | Day 61–90 is built on false assumptions |

## Key Principle

> *Onboarding a Technical Lead is not orientation. It is a deliberate accountability transfer — from the Director to the TL — that must be structured, tracked, and completed. A TL who is not fully accountable for their domain by Day 90 is a TL who has not been onboarded. They have been observed.*
